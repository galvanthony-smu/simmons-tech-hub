ATSD Communication Strategy (Simmons)

The Front Door: Tech Hub Websites

Every ATSD should have a Front Door — a Tech Hub site that reflects who we are, what we do, and how to reach us. It should look and sound like our school and be the clear place to start when someone needs help.

  • Personalized with photo, contact info, and services offered.
  • Acts as the permanent home for newsletter content and updates.
  • Provides faculty with a single point of entry into OIT’s ecosystem.

Proactive Relationship Management

Our value is built on trust. Regular 1:1s with the Dean and key leaders, ongoing meetings with program and center heads, and consistent, proactive communication keep us visible and connected.

  • We still handle the day-to-day triage and quick-turn requests.
  • But proactive engagement gives us the insight to anticipate needs.
  • If we’re not the person the Dean calls when their desk is on fire, our plan doesn’t matter.

"All ATSD" Projects — Building a Collaborative Culture

ATS Skills & Services Index

We normalize consultation and lean on each other’s strengths. The index maps all of ATS — ATSDs plus RTS, Instructional Systems, and OPS — so we can see our collective capabilities at a glance.

  • Clarifies who to consult for specific needs.
  • Helps define each school’s Tech Hub focus — what we emphasize, not what we exclude.
  • Goal: A living catalog of skills and services that supports faster handoffs and better collaboration.

AI Learning Fair & Knowledge Center

Establish ATS as the center of AI knowledge at SMU. Instead of roadshows, we host the campus: workshops, booths, and applied AI demos in Moody and the atrium.

  • Interactive sessions led by ATS and faculty innovators.
  • Vendors, demos, and faculty use cases create buzz and visibility.
  • Goal: Make AI approachable and position ATS as its academic home base.

Faculty Innovators Showcase

We recruit faculty who are doing meaningful work with technology — ideally supported by ATS — to share with peers across schools.

  • Cross-pollinates ideas that can scale across disciplines.
  • Uses “social proof” to drive adoption and surface new Champion projects.
  • Goal: Build community around innovation, anchored by the schools.

With OIT moving off-site, ATS becomes the Front Door on campus — the people faculty know, trust, and call first.

ATS Structure: Champions + Specialists

Champions (Front Door to OIT)

  • Serve as the strategic partner and single point of contact for Deans.
  • Champion school-specific initiatives that can scale across SMU.
  • Lead All-ATSD projects that strengthen ATS’s campus presence.

Specialist Hub & Index

  • Skills & Services Index connects all of ATS (ATSDs + RTS + Instructional Systems + OPS).
  • Consult-first culture: no one must be SME on everything; we route to strengths.
  • Shared platform expertise for consistency and speed (Canvas, Zoom, Panopto, Qualtrics, …).

Model Principles

  • Engine of Innovation: incubate locally in schools, then scale thoughtfully across SMU.
  • Front Door, Always: Deans know one name; we handle the rest through the hub.
  • Time to Lead: the Index offloads reactive work so Champions can drive initiatives.
  • Consult as a Norm: second/third opinions are expected; we lean on shared expertise.

Athletics Spotlight (Looking Ahead)

A distinct Champion placement serving high-visibility programs and senior leadership (AD, flagship coaches).

  • Different cadence and culture; higher public visibility.
  • Same model: trusted Front Door + shared Specialist Hub.
  • Demonstrates how the federated structure adapts to context.

Federated by design: We champion inside our schools and scale outside — sustaining innovation through shared capability and local insight.
