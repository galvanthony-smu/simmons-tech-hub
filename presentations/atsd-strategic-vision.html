Every ATSD Needs a Front Door

The Hub as Presence

The school-specific Tech Hub is the ATSD’s visible, branded entry point — the Front Door.

  • Photo + short bio; clear “who I am / what I do / how to reach me.”
  • Central, living home for services, news, presentations, and newsletters.
  • Answers live here so you can reply with links — demonstrating readiness.
  • Not about forcing self-service; it’s about showing you’ve thought of everything — your utility belt.

The Human Layer

The Front Door supports relationships — it doesn’t replace them.

  • Regular 1:1s with Deans/Associate Deans; ongoing touchpoints with program and center leads.
  • Proactive discovery of needs; follow-through that delivers results.
  • Hallway chats and triage emails are where trust is built.
  • Ultimate goal: be the person the Dean calls when the desk is “on fire.”

"All ATSD" Projects — Building a Collaborative Culture

ATS Skills & Services Index

We normalize consultation and lean on each other’s strengths. The index maps all of ATS — ATSDs plus RTS, Instructional Systems, and OPS — so we can see our collective capabilities at a glance.

  • Clarifies who to consult for specific needs (no one has to be expert in everything).
  • Guides each school’s Tech Hub focus — not to say “we don’t do X,” but to lean into strengths.
  • Goal: A living catalog of services/skills that powers faster handoffs and better outcomes.

AI Learning Fair & Knowledge Center

Establish ATS as the center of AI knowledge at SMU. Instead of chasing roadshow invites, we bring the campus to us.

  • Ongoing workshops in Moody classrooms + an atrium fair with demos, activities, and vendors.
  • Faculty share applied examples; ATS curates resources and best practices in our Hubs.
  • Goal: Make AI adoption accessible, credible, and visible — anchored by ATS.

Faculty Innovators Showcase

We recruit faculty in each school who are doing meaningful, practical things with technology (ideally supported by ATS) and have them show their work to peers across schools.

  • Cross-pollinates ideas that can scale; creates social proof and momentum.
  • Goal: Celebrate faculty, surface replicable practices, and seed new Champion projects.

With most of OIT moving off-site, ATS doubles down on being the Front Door on campus — the team people see, know, and call when the desk is on fire.

ATS Structure: Champions + Specialists

Champions (Front Door to OIT)

  • Single point of contact and strategic partner for Deans; the connective tissue into OIT.
  • Champion school-specific initiatives that can scale university-wide when they prove out.
  • Lead and represent All-ATSD projects on campus, reinforcing our primacy as the Front Door.

Specialist Hub & Index

  • Skills & Services Index maps all of ATS (ATSDs + RTS + Instructional Systems + OPS).
  • Consult-first culture: no one must be SME on everything; we route to strengths.
  • Shared platform expertise (Canvas, Zoom, Panopto, Qualtrics, …) for consistency and speed.

Model Principles

  • Engine of Innovation: incubate locally in schools, then scale thoughtfully across SMU.
  • Front Door, Always: Deans know one name; we handle the rest through the hub.
  • Time to Lead: the Index offloads reactive work so Champions have bandwidth to drive initiatives.

Federated by design: we champion inside our schools and scale outside — remaining the first call when the “desk is on fire.”

Looking ahead: ATSD for Athletics. A distinct Champion placement serving high-visibility programs and stakeholders (AD, flagship coaches). Different cadence and culture — same model: trusted Front Door, shared Specialist Hub, scalable innovation.
